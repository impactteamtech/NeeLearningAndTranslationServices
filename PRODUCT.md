# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Professionals and organizations needing Haitian Creole–English translation for legal, medical, and business documents. They arrive looking for credible, established language services — not a side-project or a hobbyist tutor. They want fast turnaround, domain expertise, and proof of quality. Secondary audience: adult learners and families seeking structured Creole/English courses with cultural context.

## Product Purpose

Nee's Learning & Translation Services bridges the Haitian Creole–English language gap for professionals and communities. The site exists to convert visitors into clients by communicating competence, cultural fluency, and reliability. Success means a visitor understands the full scope of services (language courses, document translation, cultural integration) within 30 seconds and finds a clear path to get started.

## Brand Personality

Bold, modern, educational. The voice is confident but never cold — it speaks with authority on language and culture while staying approachable. The Haitian flag colors (navy and red) anchor the identity, projecting pride and heritage alongside professional polish.

## Anti-references

- Cold, corporate enterprise translation agency pages — sterile layouts, stock imagery of handshakes and headsets, blue-gray palettes stripped of all cultural identity
- Generic SaaS landing page templates — hero-metric grids, identical card rows, gradient text headlines that could belong to any product
- Overly playful or childish language-school sites that undermine the professional translation arm

## Design Principles

1. **Heritage as identity, not decoration** — Haitian culture and flag colors are embedded in the design system, not tacked on as accent. Every visual choice should feel like it belongs to this brand specifically.
2. **Authority through clarity** — Professional services need to feel trustworthy at first glance. Typography hierarchy, clean layout, and purposeful whitespace project competence.
3. **Show the scope** — The three service pillars (learning, translation, cultural integration) should each feel substantial and distinct, not afterthoughts beneath a hero section.
4. **Earn attention, don't demand it** — Motion and visual richness should reward the visitor's attention, not compete for it. Let the content breathe.
5. **One clear path forward** — Every section resolves toward action. No dead ends.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AAA compliance. Body text contrast ≥ 7:1 against background. Large text ≥ 4.5:1. Full keyboard navigation. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations. Screen reader–friendly semantics. Color is never the sole information carrier.
